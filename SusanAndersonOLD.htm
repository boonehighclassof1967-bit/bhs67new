Update 2017:

Since 2011, I have moved once again. We definitely didn’t like living in a homeowner’s association. Both of my sons are doing well. My mother is still going strong after a triple bypass surgery last year at 87 years old. I head to Fort Worth every chance I can to visit my son, Cody, and granddaughter, Maile. Larry and I love to golf, listen to music at the numerous wineries in Iowa, spoiling our granddaughter, and travel. He was a drummer in high school and has taken that noisy hobby up again a few years ago! I have been researching my family’s genealogy since about 2002 and found interesting stories and histories. And I just started a new wellness exercise program. I hope to see everyone at the 50th reunion.

Tired of spending vacations in Iowa, I decided to move back to the Des Moines area so I could vacation somewhere else. After moving 4 times in 10 years, I told my husband, "I have one move left in me and that's home". Chris' health has been rocky so I needed to be back in Iowa where I could monitor it. With a referral from the gentleman who owned the first brokerage firm I worked for in 1983, I accepted an offer from American Equity Investment Life Insurance to come aboard and get their brokerage firm (American Equity Capital) up and running. In 2011, I left American Equity and took a position with Sammons Securities Company in West Des Moines. By the way, there's no place like home, Toto.

Update 2007
Occupation: Vice President, Admin. Mgr., Merrill Lynch
Spouse/Sig Other: Larry
Children: Christopher 39, Cody 29
Life History: Most of the 10 years after graduation was spent determining Chris’s disability level and supporting him. During those 10 years, I spent two of them in the San Francisco and Los Angeles area where I worked for an advertising agency and modeled. After marrying in 1975, Cody’s birth in 1977, and divorcing in 1983, I became a broker and moved to Austin, TX, in 1991, working for Merrill Lynch. Since that time, I have moved on to management and have lived in Des Moines, Atlanta, Baton Rouge, Beaumont, Texas and now in Valparaiso, Indiana. My career has taken me to places, heights, and dreams I never knew I had. These being entirely different from what I thought I wanted fresh out of high school. To determine Chris’s disability level has taken me through the Mayo Clinic, University of Iowa Hospitals, and numerous doctors. He is now residing in residential housing in Nevada, Iowa. Cody is married and living in Fort Worth, Texas. After being diagnosed with a kidney disease, thyroid disease, and osteoporosis, I have re-evaluated most of what I consider valuable in my life. Work is not one of them. It’s just a way to the means. I have been very lucky to have life-long friends, wonderful children, a supporting family, a great husband, a career I like, and insight to know when I’m off balance with these. From graduation to the present - It went too fast! I’ve lived in four different states for extended periods of time and there is still no place like Iowa!
